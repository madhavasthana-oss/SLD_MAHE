\label{sec:results}

Training ran on a Google Colab T4 GPU. Session time limits meant we only got 3 epochs 
instead of the configured 200. The numbers below reflect that constrained run.

We used batch size 128, SGD (Nesterov) optimizer, and the cosine annealing + warm-up 
schedule from Section~IV. Table~\ref{tab:results} has the final numbers.

Test accuracy reached \textbf{99.80\%} in just 3 epochs. Epoch 1 val accuracy was 64.41\%; 
by epoch 3 it was 99.86\%. That's a big jump. But it's worth being honest about what 
these numbers mean — training was cut short of the configured maximum, and the dataset 
itself is fairly constrained: controlled backgrounds, consistent framing, one hand per 
image. The high accuracy reflects the ImageNet backbone's strong prior as much as anything 
else. It's not a claim of general robustness.

W\&B logs showed mean gradient norm of 1.64 and clipping ratio of 0.77 at the final epoch. 
The clipping constraint was still active and the optimization trajectory was still steep 
at termination. A full run would likely give marginal gains on this dataset but would 
be more useful for evaluating harder, real-world conditions.

The main challenge for actual deployment is generalization — skin tones, lighting, 
cluttered backgrounds that the Kaggle dataset doesn't cover. The two-stage design 
helps somewhat by using the MediaPipe ROI crop to isolate the hand from background noise 
before classification.
